Who is Red Velvet?

Debut

Red Velvet is a kpop girl group that debuted under SM Entertainment on August 1, 2014 with the song Happiness. Originally debuting with four members: Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, and Joy, a fifth member, Yeri, joined in March 2015 for the release of their first comeback. Their fans are called reveluvs.

Music and Concept

Red Velvet has a dual concept that they follow musically and aesthetically. Every release generally falls under one of two categories: red and velvet. Their red side represents a bright, sometimes experimental, pop sound with colorful, bubbly, summery, and/or eccentric visuals. The velvet side, on the other hand, shows a darker, classier, and more mature image. Their velvet music consists of ballads and R&B. They sometimes also combine the two sides to make for a more unique, well rounded product. Their versatility and strength with both sides helps them stand out in the industry.

Impact

Red Velvet is known as one of the big three girl groups of kpop's third generation (around 2012-2018), along with Blackpink and Twice. They have recieved many awards and accolades over the years. For example, they rank sixth in amount of music show wins with 84 to date, ranking among icons such as BTS and Girls Generation. The have also recieved 59 total wins at various award shows. Additionally, they were the first kpop group to perform in North Korea in a diplomatic concert.
