Notable Moments in Red Velvet's Career

Debut

August 1, 2014

Red Velvet debuted with the song Happiness. Four of the members, Irene, Seulgi, Wendy, and Yeri were part of SM Rookies, a pre-debut training team who would post content showing off their talents, before debut. They were SM's first girl group to debut in five years, after f(x), who debuted in 2009.

Yeri Joins

March 16, 2015

SM announced on March 10 that a fifth member, Yeri would be joining the group. She officially made her debut with the group with the release of their song Ice Cream Cake.

First Win

March 27, 2015

Red Velvet received their first music show win with Ice Cream Cake on Music Bank. A music show is a live TV show that typically airs weekly. Kpop acts perform on stage in these shows to promote their new music. Every week, a song that's being promoted at the time wins an award based on metrics such as voting, streaming, sales, etc.

First Promotions in the States

August 2, 2015

Red Velvet performed in the United States for the first time at KCON LA. KCON is an annual kpop convention that has been held in various countries around the world, primarily in the US, along with Australia, Japan, the UAE, and more. The convention includes a concert with many groups performing.

Announcment of Fanclub Name

April 26, 2017

On the group's 1000 day anniversary, they announced their official fanclub name, ReVeluv.

First Concert

August 18, 2017-March 29, 2018

Red Velvet kicked off their first official concert titled Red Room in Seoul, South Korea. This was their first Asia Tour, with two dates in South Korea and two in Japan.

Performance in North Korea

April 1, 2018

Red Velvet performed at an inter-Korean concert titled Spring is Coming in Pyongyang, North Korea. They were SM's first group to perform in North Korea since Shinhwa, who had performed their 15 years prior. Joy was unable to attend due to scheduling conflicts as she was busy filming for the show Tempted.

Japanese Debut

July 4, 2018

Red Velvet made their Japanese debut with the song #CookieJar.

First World Tour

August 4, 2018-February 21, 2019

Red Velvet's second concert and first world tour titled Redmare started in Asia, with dates in South Korea, Thailand, Taiwan, and Singapore. They then performed in a number of cities in the US and Canada. In January, they held an associated Japanese arena tour, also titled Redmare.

First Perfect All Kill

August 8, 2018

Red Velvet achieved their first perfect all kill (PAK) with their song Power Up two days after its release. A perfect all kill occurs when a song simultaneously hits number one on the real-time, daily, and weekly charts on iChart, which aggregates all of the major music charts in Korea.

Wendy's Accident

December 25, 2019

On Christmas day, Wendy was in an accident at rehearsal for 2019 SBS Gayo Daejeon, a year end music festival. She fell around 2.5 meters off a platform and onto the stage, resulting in her breaking her pelvis, wrist, and cheekbone. She spent the next year or so mostly on hiatus as she recovered from her injuries.

Subunit and Solo Debuts

July 6, 2020-October 4, 2022

Since Wendy was unable to fully participate with the group while she recovered, the members focused on solo and subunit activities. The aptly named subunit Red Velvet - Irene & Seulgi debuted with the song Monster in the summer of 2020. In April of 2021 Wendy made her solo debut with Like Water. Joy made her solo debut the following month with Hello. Red Velvet returned to group promotions in August of that year. Seulgi had her solo debut late in 2022.

Debut of GOT the Beat

January 3, 2022

GOT the beat is a project supergroup with seven artists under SM Entertainment. The group consists of a soloist, BoA, Taeyeon and Hyoyeon from Girls Generation, Wendy and Seulgi from Red Velvet, and Winter and Karina from Aespa. They debuted with the song Step Back and released an EP Stamp on It a year later.
